What is Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)?

Coronary Artery Disease is a condition where plaque buildup narrows the coronary arteries, reducing blood flow to the heart muscle. This can lead to chest pain (angina) and heart attacks.

Did you know? CAD is the leading cause of death globally. However, with early detection and lifestyle changes, it can be managed effectively.

Risk Factors for Heart Disease

Controllable Risk Factors:

  • Smoking and alcohol consumption
  • High blood pressure (Hypertension)
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity and lack of physical activity
  • Poor diet
  • Chronic stress

Non-Controllable Risk Factors:

  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Gender (men are at higher risk earlier in life)
  • Family history of heart disease
  • Genetics

Symptoms of Heart Disease

Early recognition of symptoms can save lives. Common symptoms include:

  • Chest pain or discomfort (especially during exertion)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Pain in neck, jaw, or shoulders
  • Nausea or dizziness
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Cold sweats
⚠️ Emergency Warning: If you experience severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, or loss of consciousness, call emergency services immediately (Dial 911 or your local emergency number).

Prevention & Healthy Lifestyle

Dietary Recommendations:

  • Eat heart-healthy foods: fruits, vegetables, whole grains
  • Reduce sodium and saturated fats
  • Limit sugar and refined carbohydrates
  • Include fish rich in omega-3 fatty acids
  • Stay hydrated with plenty of water

Exercise & Activity:

  • Aim for 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week
  • Include strength training 2-3 times per week
  • Avoid sedentary lifestyle
  • Take regular breaks if working long hours

Lifestyle Changes:

  • Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Manage stress through meditation or yoga
  • Get 7-9 hours of quality sleep
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Regular health checkups

Heart Disease Testing & Diagnosis

Common Tests Include:

  • Blood tests: Check cholesterol and other markers
  • ECG (Electrocardiogram): Measures heart's electrical activity
  • Stress test: Monitor heart during physical exertion
  • Echocardiogram: Ultrasound of the heart
  • CT scan: Detailed images of heart and blood vessels
  • Angiography: X-ray to visualize arteries
Regular screening is important! Adults over 40 should have regular health checkups to monitor cardiovascular health.

When to See a Doctor

Schedule an appointment with a healthcare provider if you:

  • Experience chest pain or discomfort
  • Feel persistent shortness of breath
  • Have a family history of heart disease
  • Have risk factors like high blood pressure or diabetes
  • Want to discuss preventive measures
  • Have been diagnosed with heart disease
Seek Emergency Care if you experience: Severe chest pain, sudden severe shortness of breath, loss of consciousness, or symptoms of a heart attack.
